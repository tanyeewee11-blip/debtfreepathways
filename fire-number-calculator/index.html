FIRE calculator FAQs
Your FIRE number is the portfolio size needed to sustain your desired annual spending indefinitely, calculated by dividing your annual spending by your chosen withdrawal rate (commonly 4%, which is the same as multiplying spending by 25).
Lean FIRE typically targets annual spending under $40,000 and a smaller portfolio, while Fat FIRE targets $80,000-$120,000 or more in annual spending and a correspondingly larger portfolio. Both use the same underlying withdrawal-rate math, just applied to a different lifestyle.
4% is the traditional starting point based on the Trinity Study, designed for a 30-year retirement. Because early retirees often face 40- to 50-year time horizons, many use a more conservative 3% to 3.5% withdrawal rate instead, which requires a larger portfolio for the same spending.
Not separately — healthcare should be included in your annual spending estimate. Early retirees typically need to budget an extra line item for health insurance premiums before Medicare eligibility at 65, since this is one of the most commonly underestimated costs in early retirement planning.

How This FIRE Number Calculator Works

This calculator divides your desired annual retirement spending by your chosen withdrawal rate to arrive at your FIRE number — the portfolio size needed to sustain that spending indefinitely. It then works forward from your current invested assets and monthly contribution, compounded monthly at your expected return, to estimate how many years it will take to reach that number and at what age.

Why the Withdrawal Rate Matters So Much

A lower withdrawal rate produces a larger FIRE number for the same spending, because it assumes a longer or more conservative retirement horizon. Moving from a 4% to a 3.5% withdrawal rate increases your required portfolio by about 14% — worth checking against your own risk tolerance and expected retirement length rather than defaulting to 4% automatically.

Why We Show Lean, Your Number, and Fat FIRE Together

FIRE isn't one target — it's a spectrum of lifestyles. Seeing how many years separate a Lean FIRE number from a Fat FIRE number, holding your savings rate constant, makes the real trade-off visible: a leaner lifestyle gets you there faster, but a fatter one gives you more room to live comfortably once you arrive.
